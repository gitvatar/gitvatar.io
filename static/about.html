gitvatar: ('gi-vah-tahr)—the first "t" is silent.

gitvatar is like Gravatar (Globally Recognized Avatars), except the uploaded avatar images are stored in a public Github repository. Each uploaded avatar image is uniquely and permanently identified by its own Git commit hash.

Don't worry, though. Your email address is not stored in the public repository in any way, only your avatar image. Your email address is only stored privately by our service, and only in hashed format—thus unreadable and unusable by spammers! For more information, see our Privacy Policy and our Terms of Service.

Avatar images are served through Github's raw.githubusercontent.com CDN service.

gitvatar's API supports both uploading and viewing of avatar images. This makes gitvatar a little more flexible/powerful for developers to integrate into their applications than something like Gravatar.
